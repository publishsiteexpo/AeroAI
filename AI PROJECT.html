Problem Scoping

Bullet trains travelling above 300 km/h experience rapidly increasing aerodynamic drag, leading to higher energy consumption. AeroAI predicts a streamlined nose design to theoretically reduce this drag.

  • Who → Railway Engineers
  • What → Increased energy consumption
  • Where → High-speed bullet train systems
  • Why → Support sustainable transportation

Data Acquisition

Generated a theoretical dataset of 1,000 aerodynamic samples using physics equations. Dataset includes speed, nose angle, air density, and drag coefficient.

  • Velocity
  • Air Density
  • Shape Angle
  • Drag Coefficient

Data Exploration

Cleaned the dataset by removing unrealistic values. Observed that drag increases rapidly as train speed increases.

  • Data Cleaning

Data Modelling

A Regression Model predicts the ideal train nose shape that minimizes drag.
